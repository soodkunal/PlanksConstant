Experiment: Photoelectric Effect

In this experiment, you will investigate the photoelectric effect and explore the relationship between the intensity of light and the photoelectric current.

Apparatus Required

  • Light source (e.g., LED)
  • Power supply
  • Photocell

THEORY:

Planck's constant is a fundamental constant in quantum mechanics, denoted by the symbol h. It relates the energy of a photon to its frequency through the equation: E = h * f where E is the energy, h is Planck's constant, and f is the frequency of the photon. Idealy the value of Planck's constant is approximately 6.62607015 × 10-34 joule-seconds (J·s) or kilogram-square meters per second (kg·m2/s). Planck's constant plays a crucial role in quantum mechanics, as it determines the quantization of energy levels and the behavior of particles at the atomic and subatomic scales. It is a fundamental constant that underlies many phenomena in physics. Planck's constant was first introduced by the German physicist Max Planck in 1900 while studying black-body radiation. His discovery paved the way for the development of quantum theory and revolutionized our understanding of the microscopic world. Planck's constant is used in various areas of physics, including quantum mechanics, particle physics, and spectroscopy.
